Predict your Income based on Census Data in Minutes!

Our machine learning model trained to predict an individual's income category (<=50K or >50K) based on various features. The model uses a dataset from the UCI Census Income dataset to make the predictions. The project is built with Python, Scikit-learn, and Flask, with a front-end built using HTML and CSS.

Understanding the Key Attributes for Prediction

In this census income prediction model, several key attributes influence the final prediction of the income. Each attribute plays an important role in determining the quality, value, and overall pricing of the gemstone. Here's a brief explanation of each input:

Age

The Age of the Individual(numerical value).

Workclass

Type of employment the person is in (e.g., Private, Federal-gov, Self-employed, etc.).

Education

Highest level of education achieved (e.g., Bachelors, Masters, Doctorate, etc.).

Occupation

Type of job the person does (e.g., Tech-support, Craft-repair, Sales, etc.).

Hours Worked Per Week

The number of hours worked per week by the individual.

Gender

Gender of the individual (Male or Female).

Capital Gain

The amount of capital gains earned by the individual (numerical value, often from investment).

Capital Loss

The amount of capital losses incurred by the individual (numerical value, often from investment).

Relationship

The relationship status of the individual (e.g., Husband, Wife, Not-in-family, etc.).

Race

The race of the individual (e.g., White, Black, Asian-Pac-Islander, etc.).
